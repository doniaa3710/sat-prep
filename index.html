About The SATs


Whether you are a Freshman or Junior, acing the SATs is the best way to succeed! Below are a few tips and guidelines to improve your overall grade.


Taking the SATs is a helpful tool colleges use to determine if you are a good fit for there school. Although many may not agree with this system of acceptance, we must make the most out of it.

Reading Section


Strategy 1: Switch It Up

If you realize you donâ€™t have enough time to finish the passage or answer the question, here are a few tips:

  • Skim the passage on the first read. Don't try to understand every single line or what the questions might ask. Just get a general understanding of the passage.
  • Next, go to the questions. If the question refers to a certain line number, go back to that line number and work on understanding the text around it.
  • If you can't answer a question within 30 seconds, skip it.


Strategy 2: Find Your Reading Skill Weaknesses and Work On Them

For every hour you study for the SAT, it needs to be the most effective hour possible.

  • Big Picture/Main Point
  • Inference
  • Vocabulary in Context
  • Author Technique
  • Evidence Support
  • Data Interpretation
  • Analyzing Multiple Texts
  • Don't Focus On Vocab


Strategy 3: Only Use High-Quality SAT Reading Sources
  • To get enough SAT Reading practice, you'll need to use other materials, too.
  • For every question you miss, identify what type of question it is. When you notice patterns to the questions you miss, you must then devote extra practice to those sub-skills.


Writing Section


Strategy 1: You MUST Practice Your Grammar Rules
  • Punctuation
  • Sentence Structure
  • Conventional Expression
  • Possessives
  • Agreement
  • Parallel Structure
  • Modifiers
  • Pronouns
  • Verb Tense


Strategy 2: Do a Ton of Practice, and Understand Every Single Mistake
  • Every mistake you make on a test happens for a reason. If you don't understand exactly why you missed that question, you will make that mistake over and over again.


Strategy 3: Be Careful With NO CHANGE Answers
  • In SAT Writing, most questions have a NO CHANGE option.
  • The SAT loves tricking students using these answer choices, because it knows that students who don't know grammar rules won't see anything wrong with the sentence.


Math- No Calculator Section

Strategy 1: Understand Your Weakness
  • Basic Algebra
    • Linear functions
    • Single variable equations
    • Systems of linear equations
    • Absolute value
  • Advanced Algebra
    • Polynomials
    • Quadratic equations
    • Exponential functions
    • Solving exponential equations
  • Problem Solving and Data Analysis
    • Ratios and proportions
    • Scatter Plots and graphs
    • Categorical data and probabilities
    • Experimental interpretation
    • Median, median, mode, standard deviation


Strategy 2: If You Miss a Question, Try It Again Before Moving on
  • try to re-solve the question once over again and try to get the correct answer.
  • Your goal at the end of all this work is to get so good at SAT Math that you solve every question and have extra time left over at the end of the section to recheck your work


Strategy 3: Memorize the Formulas and Common Math Facts
  • If you're still flipping to the front of the section to look at the math formulas, you haven't gotten to understand SAT Math well enough yet.
  • Learning to solve questions without the use of a calculator will strengthen your math intuition and force you to understand the underlying math, rather than relying on a calculator


Strategy 4: Bring a Calculator (Section 4)
  • Since you are allowed to use a calculator, why not!
  • Depending on either the regular or graphing calculator, search up tips and hacks on what each calculator is good for
